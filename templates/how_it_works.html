Your Workflow

Prompt → AI → Resources → Class

1

Enter Your Teaching Prompt

Tell EduRipple what you need in simple, plain language. No special formatting required.

Example prompts you can use:

  • "Grade 8 English lesson plan on narrative writing—60 minutes"
  • "Form 1 Biology scheme of work for Term 1—photosynthesis focus"
  • "Create a rubric for assessing group presentations in Social Studies"
  • "I need teaching resources for Grade 7 mathematics—fractions and decimals"

Tip: The more specific you are, the better EduRipple can tailor the output to your needs.

2

AI Aligns to CBC Structure

EduRipple processes your request and maps it to Kenya's CBC framework automatically.

What EduRipple organizes for you:

  • ✓ Strand and sub-strand alignment
  • ✓ Specific learning outcomes (SLOs)
  • ✓ Digital literacy and 21st-century competencies
  • ✓ Link to national values and life skills
  • ✓ Suggested teaching/learning activities

Result: Every resource respects CBC standards and classroom reality.

3

Receive Ready-to-Use Materials

EduRipple generates structured, classroom-ready resources in seconds.

Lesson Plans

Complete with timing, activities, assessment

Schemes of Work

Term-by-term or annual planning

Assessments & Rubrics

Benchmarks and grading criteria

Resource Links

Videos, texts, and teaching aids

Review the output in the chat. Ask clarifying questions or request edits anytime.

4

Review, Download & Reuse

Save your resources to the library and build a personal teaching toolkit.

What you can do next:

  • ✓ Download as PDF for printing
  • ✓ Edit in Word or Google Docs for customization
  • ✓ Save to your library for future use
  • ✓ Reuse and adapt year after year
  • ✓ Share with colleagues (if needed)

Everything is saved in your library. Come back anytime to regenerate or modify resources.
